## 2. Problem Statement
 
### The Business Problem
 
The company cannot trust its own inventory numbers. At any given moment, the quantity shown in the system does not match what is physically available in the warehouse. This creates a cascade of operational failures that directly impact revenue, customer experience, and purchasing decisions.
 
**This is not a data quality problem. It is a systems and governance problem.**
 
### Observed Operational Reality
 
A recurring scenario at the wholesale branches:
 
> A business customer arrives to collect an order. The POS staff creates a draft order based on system stock. The customer takes the paper draft and physically walks to the warehouse. The warehouse team picks what is available — but the system shows 55 units while only 40 are actually on the shelf. The picker crosses out 15 units manually. The customer walks back to POS. The invoice is generated for the adjusted quantity. The system is still not updated. The cycle repeats.
 
This scenario happened multiple times daily across branches.
 
### Financial & Operational Impact
 
| Impact Area | Observed Symptom | Estimated Frequency |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Lost sales | Customer orders items not physically available | Daily |
| Overstock | Procurement orders based on inflated system counts | Weekly |
| Customer dissatisfaction | 3-step physical journey per transaction | Every wholesale transaction |
| Staff inefficiency | Manual reconciliation to align system vs. physical | Daily (end of shift) |
| Financial misreporting | Invoices don't align with actual dispatched quantities | Every non-standard transaction |
